Data, Information, and Knowledge

ICT Curriculum
Grade 10 – Egypt

What is Data?

Definition: Data are raw facts, figures, or symbols that have not yet been processed to give them meaning.

Examples:

23
Numbers
"Ahmed"
Words
#@$
Symbols
  • A list of temperatures: 30, 32, 29
  • Names in a class: Sara, Omar, Youssef
  • Random numbers: 100, 3.14, 42

What is Information?

Definition: Information is data that has been processed or organized to have meaning and value.

Examples:

  • "Ahmed scored 23 marks in math."
  • "The average temperature this week is 30°C."
  • A table showing students' names and their scores
  • A weather report summarizing daily temperatures
Key Point: Information = Data + Context + Meaning

What is Knowledge?

Definition: Knowledge is the understanding and awareness gained by using information to make decisions or solve problems.

Examples:

  • Knowing that studying regularly improves exam scores
  • Realizing that high temperatures mean you should drink more water
  • A teacher uses students' scores to decide who needs extra help
  • Ahmed chooses to wear light clothes because he knows it will be hot

Characteristics of Information

Persistence

Hard to erase completely

Example: A photo posted online

Reproducibility

Can be copied and shared easily

Example: Forwarding a message

Spreads Easily

Can reach many people quickly

Example: Viral news

Real Example: A funny video shared on social media is seen by thousands in minutes.

Types of Information

Type Description Example
Primary Direct from the original source Survey results, eyewitness account
Secondary Processed or summarized from primary sources News article, research report

Additional Example:

  • Primary: Interview with a scientist
  • Secondary: Article summarizing the interview

Data Processing

Definition: The act of converting data into information by organizing, sorting, or analyzing it.
Collect Data
Process Data
Create Information

Examples:

  • Calculating the average score from a list of marks
  • Sorting names alphabetically
  • Making a chart from survey results
  • Analyzing sales figures to identify trends

Information Lifecycle

1. Data Collection

Data is collected

2. Processing

Data is processed into information

3. Knowledge

Information is used to gain knowledge

Example:

  • Step 1: Collecting students' marks (data)
  • Step 2: Calculating class average (information)
  • Step 3: Deciding who needs help (knowledge)

Media

Definition: Tools or methods used to express, transmit, and store information.

Types & Examples:

  • Expression media: Text, images, sound, video (writing a school report, recording a song)
  • Transmission media: TV, radio, Internet (WhatsApp, watching news)
  • Recording media: Paper, USB drive, cloud storage (saving homework on a flash drive)
Ahmed records a voice note on his phone (expression), sends it via WhatsApp (transmission), and keeps a copy on his phone (recording).

Media Literacy

Definition: The ability to understand, interpret, and evaluate information in different media.

Examples:

  • Reading a news article online and checking if it is true by comparing sources.
  • Sara sees a post on social media about a new school rule; she asks her teacher to verify it.

Information Ethics

Definition: Guidelines for responsible use and behavior with information online and offline.

Examples:

  • Respecting privacy (not sharing friends' info without permission)
  • Not copying others’ work (avoiding plagiarism in assignments)
  • Not spreading rumors or fake news
Omar finds an answer key for an exam online but chooses not to share it because it’s unfair.

Digital Age Risks

Definition: Dangers connected to technology use.

Main Risks:

  • Cyberbullying: Sending mean messages online
  • Internet addiction: Spending too much time online/games
  • Privacy issues: Accidentally sharing personal photos publicly
  • Identity theft: Someone uses your account without permission
Youssef gets a friend request from a stranger and asks his parents before accepting.

Real-World Example: Ahmed's Week

Scenario:

📊 Data:

Ahmed records the daily temperature: 30°C, 32°C, 35°C, 33°C, 34°C, 36°C, 31°C

📈 Information:

He calculates the weekly average: 33°C

He identifies the hottest day: Saturday (36°C)

💡 Knowledge:

He decides to go swimming on Saturday (the hottest day)

He understands the week was generally hot and plans accordingly

Model Answers

1. Definitions:
  • Data: Raw facts or figures
  • Information: Processed data with meaning
  • Knowledge: Understanding gained from information
2. Characteristics: Information is persistent, reproducible, and spreads easily in the digital age.
3. Types: Primary info comes directly from the source; secondary info is summarized from primary sources.
4. Example: Collecting students' marks (data) and calculating the average (information).
5. Persistence: Once created (especially digitally), information is hard to erase fully.
6. Lifecycle: Data is collected → processed into information → used to gain knowledge.
